Welcome to Academics at South Florida State College

South Florida State College offers three major tracks: the Bachelor of Applied Science degree, the Bachelor of Science degree, the Associate in Arts degree for university transfer, and technical workforce programs leading to an Associate in Science degree, certificate, or diploma.

For baccalaureate programs, both the Bachelor of Applied Science and Bachelor of Science degrees require students to complete an associate degree (either Associate in Arts or Associate in Science) prior to admission.

The Associate in Arts degree is the primary degree for transfer to other colleges and universities. Students in this program complete 36 credit hours of general education courses required by all state colleges and universities and customize other course selections with specific electives required by the transfer institution. Completion of the Associate in Arts degree guarantees admission to Florida colleges or universities as a junior-level student, though it does not guarantee admission to a specific program at the four-year institution. Students should work closely with the Advising and Counseling Center and transfer institution advisors when selecting coursework.

South Florida State College offers a variety of workforce programs. Associate in Science programs, typically two years in length, prepare students for workforce entry and may transfer to four-year institutions. Other workforce programs, one year or less, provide training for immediate job entry, including College Credit Certificates, Career Certificates, and Apprenticeships.
